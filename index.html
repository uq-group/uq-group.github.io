Hadi Meidani

I am an Associate Professor in the Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering, and Department of Biomedical & Translational Sciences at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. I got my Ph.D. in Civil Engineering from USC under the advice of Prof. Roger Ghanem. I also got a M.Sc. in Electrical Engineering from USC, a M.S. in Structural Engineering from Sharif University of Technology and a B.S in Civil Engineering from K.N. Toosi University of Technology.

Our research focuses on scientific machine learning, uncertainty quantification, and surrogate-based modeling. Our group specializes in developing advanced computational methods that combine the power of machine learning with the rigor of physics-based modeling. Our goal is to make engineering systems more reliable, efficient, and resilient in the face of uncertainty. Applications of our work include engineering design, digital twins, biomedical problems, and infrastructure and structures.

Prospective Students: I am looking for motivated students to work on cutting-edge research in scientific machine learning and uncertainty quantification. If you are interested in working with me, please apply through the CEE department and mention my name in your application. You can also email me directly with your CV and research interests.

Research Thrusts

We believe that the future of engineering lies in the seamless integration of data-driven methods with physics-based understanding. Our group seeks to develop methods that are not only computationally efficient but also physically meaningful and interpretable.
